HOME PAGE

Here are my wireframes for my website. I have maintained this design as I noticed it was common among car websites. The reason I chose this design version was due to its popularity among car websites. DATA VISUALS PAGE

The data visualization page includes two visualizations. The first is a notebook that demonstrates using d3.interpolateZoom to implement smooth pan-and-zoom transitions between two views, which will be images of the cars from the front. Each circle represents a car as an item. Additionally, I have included a bar graph feature where the x-values are not shown, but the y-values serve as name tags for the bars. In my case, the image of the bar will be replaced with a side view of the vehicle.

Moreover, both visuals will have an extra feature: when the user's cursor hovers over an item or the image of the car in both visuals, a pop-up will display more information about the car, such as pricing, gasoline intake, and model number.

DESIGN AND THEORY PAGE

The page design is quite simple, utilizing two large graphics to fill the negative space within the page. The respective information can be accessed through a large, labeled button. The information will also be color-coded to maintain a consistent aesthetic throughout the webpage. Additionally, the information will be centered and will not touch the borders of the page to avoid making the user feel cramped.

STYLE GUIDE

BRIEF CONTEXT

Lamborghini is a luxury brand known for developing some of the most popular and expensive supercars in the world. It is often seen as a symbol of wealth. Accordingly, the aesthetics and feel I aim to execute are those of luxury, quality, and professionalism.

TYPOGRAPHY

As mentioned earlier, the effect I am aiming for is that of luxury, making the choice of font quite important to convey a fancy vibe. However, after some deliberation, I realized that Lamborghini is not just about being fancy; it is about reliability and quality. Therefore, the font must be strong and affirm this ideology.

This conclusion was drawn from the official Lamborghini website, where the font choice conveys strength and quality rather than fanciness. The fonts I have chosen for the site are Pangchan and Satoshi. Apart from the website, I have applied design principles and decided to go with these two fonts for dynamicism.

COLOUR PALETTE

For the website's color palette, the first colors that came to mind were a dark grey and the goldish-yellow seen in the Lamborghini badge. These are the primary colors I wanted to use. Additionally, I opted for white text to effectively convey information.

GRAPHICS

The main graphics I will use are images of various Lamborghini models, as the primary purpose of my website is to sell or display these cars.

UI/UX

USER EXPERIENCE GOALS

My primary goal for this site’s user experience is to create an interface that is both visually appealing and intuitive. Given Lamborghini’s luxury brand, it’s essential that the site feels premium yet simple to navigate, allowing users to focus on the cars and not be distracted by overly complex design.

INTERACTION DESIGN

To enhance user interaction, I plan to include subtle animations, such as smooth transitions and hover effects. These will not only make the site feel more dynamic but will also guide the user’s attention to key areas, creating a seamless browsing experience that aligns with the brand’s sophisticated image.

RESPONSIVE DESIGN

Ensuring that the website is fully responsive is crucial. The design will adapt to various screen sizes, providing an optimal viewing experience on desktops, tablets, and mobile devices. This adaptability will reflect Lamborghini’s commitment to quality and performance in all conditions.

ACCESSIBILITY

Accessibility is another important aspect of the site. I will follow WCAG guidelines to ensure that users with disabilities can navigate the site effectively. This includes proper contrast ratios, keyboard navigation support, and meaningful alt text for images, which aligns with Lamborghini’s value of inclusivity.
